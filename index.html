Our story

The chain started on a stairwell, not in a kitchen.

In 1962, Teta Nour set a single gas ring on the landing outside her flat on Rue Pharaon and started feeding whoever was hungry — the ironmonger downstairs, the students two floors up, the taxi drivers who parked outside her window. She never called it a business. She called it Tuesday.

Six decades, one war, and a currency collapse later, her granddaughter Yara linked that landing to a charcoal grill three doors down and put a roof over both. She named it after the thing that made it possible: سلسلة — silsila, the chain. Every recipe here still carries the name of the hand that gave it to us.

Meet the menu that grew from it →
  1. 1962

    Teta Nour opens her landing to the stairwell, Rue Pharaon.

  2. 1979

    Through the war years, her table never once closes. Neighbors become family.

  3. 2003

    Uncle Fadi rolls a charcoal cart onto the same street — the smoke becomes a local landmark.

  4. 2016

    Yara links both kitchens under one roof and calls it Silsila.

  5. Today

    Every dish still carries a name. Pull up a chair — you're part of the chain now.
